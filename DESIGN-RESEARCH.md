# Design research: Outsourced Assistants

## Search intent

**Query:** `virtual assistant outsourcing services`

The buyer is not shopping for software or a freelance directory. They want a company that can help define a role, find a capable remote assistant, and keep the work on track after hiring.

## Five direct organic competitors inspected

### 1. Wing Assistant — wingassistant.com

- Pale blue and white SaaS-style system with blue-purple gradients.
- Very large outcome-led headline, benefit checks, two CTAs, ratings, and customer logos above the fold.
- Strong fit/not-fit framing and clear task groups farther down.
- Weaknesses: the hero has a large empty right side, proof gets crowded, and CTA wording changes repeatedly.

### 2. 10X Outsource — 10xoutsource.com

- White, navy, and bright green brand system.
- Split hero with bold copy on the left and a full-height office photo on the right.
- Simple primary CTA and a phone number provide clear next steps.
- Weaknesses: generic stock photography, crowded navigation, unsupported superlatives, and almost no proof near the hero.

### 3. Stealth Agents — stealthagents.com

- Dark navy hero with cyan type, a rounded assistant portrait, and a dense white card system below.
- Strong role taxonomy, early reviews, aggregate ratings, and a simple hiring process.
- Weaknesses: an extremely long page, repeated claims, many CTA labels, and stock-like imagery. The volume of proof makes the best evidence harder to spot.

### 4. Remote CoWorker — remotecoworker.com

- Warm cream, brown, and coral palette rather than the usual blue staffing template.
- Split hero pairs a headset-wearing assistant with small workflow cards that make the service tangible.
- Review signals and media marks sit close to the first screen, followed by profiles and a clear process.
- Weaknesses: dense navigation, competing CTA labels, and overlapping hero widgets that feel overproduced. Some lower sections appeared blank or unloaded in the inspected full-page capture.

### 5. Outsource Access — outsourceaccess.com

- Purple and black brand system with yellow CTAs and video-led explanation.
- Award badges and the founder-led message aim for authority.
- Weaknesses: the hero is copy-heavy, the navigation is crowded, overlays compete with the CTA, and the video showed a connection-security error in the inspected capture.

## Chosen direction

**Primary inspiration: Remote CoWorker.** It had the strongest niche fit because the first screen shows a person doing assistant work and supports the image with lightweight workflow cues. Its warm palette also feels more approachable than the blue SaaS look used by most VA providers.

The original direction for Outsourced Assistants is an **assistant handoff desk**: warm paper tones, dark plum type, tomato-red actions, editorial serif headlines, a locally stored team-planning image, and compact task briefs styled like notes passed between an owner and an assistant. The photo is the site's existing Unsplash-derived asset from commit `f0fc7f4`; it shows two professionals reviewing work at a table, includes no location claim, and is paired with explicit Philippines-only copy rather than an inferred identity. The homepage focuses on choosing the first workload, setting access rules, and reviewing the first two weeks.

## Patterns to reuse

- One short, outcome-led headline with one primary CTA.
- A relevant human image rather than an abstract gradient or empty hero.
- Concrete task examples before broad service claims.
- A visible onboarding sequence and clear ownership language.
- A compact planning strip near the hero, without fabricated ratings, customer logos, or performance claims.
- One consistent conversion phrase: **Request a role plan**.

## Originality notes

The implementation will not copy competitor copy, logos, illustrations, profile cards, price claims, or layouts. The note-card motif, typography, colors, workload sorter, and two-week handoff timeline are original to this site. Existing routes, metadata, legal pages, footer disclosures, copyright, and structured data remain intact.
